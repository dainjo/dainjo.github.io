BMW M-Sport 520d

Some of the 520d’s performance capabilities, and indeed its economical abilities,
come from a significant reduction in weight compared to the car it replaces - up to 100kg in some models,
though the 520d M Sport is still 70kg lighter than its direct predecessor.
An increase in high-tensile steel is mostly to thank, though increased use of aluminum and magnesium have also helped. What the new Five doesn’t have is a 7-series-style carbon core,
so you imagine there are more weight savings to come when the next-gen 5-series eventually arrives.
